Notes

Distances to points on Earth are based on great circle distances from your current position at sea level (on a WGS84 reference ellipsoid). Distance to the Moon is based on the shortest path from your current position to the lunar surface that avoids passing through the Earth. Thus if the Moon is currently above your local horizon this is a straight line to the nearest point on the lunar surface; if the Moon is currently below your local horizon it is the distance from your current position to the nearest point on Earth where the Moon is above the horizon plus the distance from there to the lunar surface, as shown in red on the diagram below.
